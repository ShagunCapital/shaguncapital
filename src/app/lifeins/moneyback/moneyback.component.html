What is Money Back Policy?

A money back plan in life insurance is a type of policy that pays out a lump sum to the policyholder at specific intervals (such as every 5 or 10 years) during the policy term, in addition to the death benefit. These payouts can be used for various purposes, such as funding children's education or covering living expenses. The policyholder continues to pay premiums throughout the term of the policy, and upon the policyholder's death, the beneficiaries receive the remaining death benefit. Money back plans provide the policyholder with a combination of savings and protection.


What all Money Back Policy Covers?

A money-back policy in life insurance typically covers the policyholder's death or the maturity of the policy. In the event of the policyholder's death, the death benefit, as stated in the policy, will be paid to the beneficiary. If the policy matures, the policyholder will receive the maturity benefit, which is the sum of all the premiums paid, plus any bonuses or interest. Some money-back policies also include a survival benefit, which pays the policyholder a percentage of the sum assured at regular intervals during the policy term. It is always recommended to go through the policy document carefully before buying a life insurance policy to understand the coverage and benefits provided by the policy.


Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for a money back policy in life insurance varies depending on the specific policy and insurance company. Generally, the policyholder must have paid all premiums on time and the policy must be in force. Some policies may have additional requirements, such as being a certain age or having a certain amount of coverage. It's best to check with the specific insurance company or review the policy details for more information on the eligibility criteria for a money back policy.


Money Back Policy Claim Process

A money-back policy in life insurance is a type of policy where the policyholder receives a portion of the sum insured at regular intervals (such as every 5 years) during the policy term, while the remaining amount is paid out at the end of the policy term or upon the death of the insured. This is in contrast to a traditional life insurance policy, where the sum insured is paid out only upon the death of the insured or at the end of the policy term. Money-back policies provide policyholders with a regular income stream, in addition to the death benefit provided by traditional life insurance policies. The claim process for a money-back policy is similar to that of a traditional life insurance policy, with the policyholder or their nominee being required to submit a claim form and any required documents to the insurer.


Documents Required for Claim Process

The documents required for the claim process of a money back policy in life insurance can vary depending on the specific policy and insurance company. However, some common documents that may be required include:

  • A death certificate or proof of death, such as a police report or coroner's report

  • The original policy document or a certified copy

  • A completed claim form, which may be obtained from the insurance company

  • Any additional documentation required by the insurance company, such as medical records or witness statements

It's important to check with the specific insurance company to determine exactly what documents will be required for the claim process.

FAQs

Q1. How does a money-back policy work?

A money-back policy typically pays out a percentage of the death benefit at specified intervals (e.g. every five or ten years) during the policyholder's lifetime. If the policyholder dies before the end of the policy term, the remaining death benefit is paid to the beneficiaries.


Q2. What are the benefits of a money-back policy?

A money-back policy provides both death benefit coverage and a return of premium payments, which can provide financial security for both the policyholder and their beneficiaries. Additionally, the return of premium payments can help policyholders meet their financial goals.


Q3. Are there any drawbacks to a money-back policy?

One potential drawback of a money-back policy is that the premiums may be higher than those for traditional life insurance policies. Additionally, if the policyholder cancels the policy or surrenders it early, they may not receive a full refund of the premium payments.


Q4. How do I make a claim on a money-back policy?

To make a claim on a money-back policy, the policyholder or their beneficiaries will typically need to provide proof of death, such as a death certificate, to the insurance company. The insurance company will then process the claim and pay out the death benefit or return of premium payments as specified in the policy.
