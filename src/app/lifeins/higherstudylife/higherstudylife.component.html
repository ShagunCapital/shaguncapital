What is Higher Study Plan?

Higher Study Plan in Life Insurance is a type of insurance policy that allows policyholders to use the death benefit to pay for their children's education expenses after they pass away. The policy typically includes a cash value component that can be used to pay for the child's education expenses, such as tuition and books, while they are still alive. The policyholder can also choose to use the cash value to pay for their own education expenses, such as continuing education or vocational training. This type of policy can be a good option for parents who want to ensure that their children have the means to pay for their education in the event of the policyholder's death.


What all Higher Study plan Covers?

Higher study plans in life insurance typically cover the cost of tuition, books, and other expenses associated with pursuing a higher education degree. These plans may also provide a cash benefit to the policyholder in the event of the insured's death or total permanent disability. Some plans also provide a maturity benefit, which is paid out upon completion of the educational program. Additionally, some higher study plans have inbuilt flexibility that allows the policyholder to adjust coverage levels as the policyholder's educational needs change over time.

Higher Study Plan Claim Process

For a hassle-free claim process, you should understand the steps to be followed for making claims. However, the process can be different for each insurance company. Here is a quick guide to understand the process.

  • In case of planned hospitalisation, inform the insurance company beforehand. In case of sudden requirement, inform the company as soon as possible

  • Submit the hospital bills and other certificates required by the insurance company. If approved, your bill will be paid directly by the company to the hospital, if it is a network hospital. In case it is a not a network hospital under the insurance company, the claims will be of reimbursement type instead of the cashless type

  • If not approved, you can submit the other required documents. In this case, you will have to pay the hospital bill which will be reimbursed later

FAQs

Q1. What do you understand by a Health Card?

A Health Card is a card provided at the time of issuing the health insurance policy. The card helps in identifying you so that you can get cashless hospitalisation at their network hospitals.


Q2. Should I opt for a separate family health plan if I am covered by my office?

A personal family health plan is always recommended so that you can get it done according to your need and requirement. In case you change that job, your corporate health plan will also end and it might happen that your new plan does not include the needed coverage.
