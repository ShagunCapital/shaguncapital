What is Corporate Health Plan?

A corporate health plan is a type of health insurance that is offered by an employer to its employees. These plans typically provide comprehensive coverage for medical expenses, including doctor visits, hospital stays, prescription drugs, and preventive care.

Employers usually offer a choice of health plans to their employees, and the employee typically pays a portion of the premium through payroll deductions. The employer may also contribute to the cost of the premium, depending on the plan. Corporate health plans can be either self-insured, meaning the employer sets aside funds to pay for employee medical expenses, or fully-insured, meaning the employer purchases insurance from an insurance company.

Corporate health plans can be preferred by employees as they are often more affordable and offer more coverage options than individual plans. They can also offer additional benefits such as wellness programs and employee assistance programs.


What all Corporate Health plan Covers?

A Corporate Health plan is a group health insurance policy that is purchased by an employer for its employees. The coverage and benefits offered by a Corporate Health plan can vary depending on the insurance provider and the specific policy. However, some common features of a Corporate Health plan are:

Hospitalization expenses: Coverage for hospitalization expenses, including room rent, doctor's fees, diagnostic tests, and surgeries.

Pre and post-hospitalization expenses: Coverage for pre and post-hospitalization expenses, including medical expenses incurred before and after hospitalization.

Daycare procedures: Coverage for daycare procedures, which are medical procedures that do not require an overnight hospital stay.

Ambulance expenses: Coverage for ambulance expenses incurred during the course of hospitalization.

Critical Illness coverage: Coverage for specified critical illnesses, such as cancer, heart attack, or stroke.

Health check-ups: Coverage for periodic health check-ups.

No-Claim Bonus: A no-claim bonus, which is a reward given to policyholders who have not made a claim during the policy term.

Cashless facilities: Cashless facilities, which allow policyholders to avail medical treatment at network hospitals without having to pay upfront.

It is important to carefully review the coverage and benefits offered by a specific Corporate Health plan to ensure that it meets the needs of the policyholder and the employees.


Eligibility Criteria

The eligibility criteria for a Corporate Health plan can vary depending on the insurance provider and the specific policy. However, here are some common eligibility criteria:

  • Employee: The policyholder must be an employee of the company purchasing the policy.

  • Age: The policyholder must be within a specified age range, typically between 18 and 65 years old.

  • Enrollment: The policyholder must enroll in the policy within a specified time frame, usually during the open enrollment period.

  • Residency: The policyholder must be a resident of India.

  • Waiting period: A waiting period may apply, during which the policyholder is not eligible to make a claim.

It is important to carefully review the eligibility criteria for a specific Corporate Health plan to ensure that the policyholder and the employees meet the requirements and are eligible to participate in the plan. If the policyholder is unsure about the eligibility criteria, they can contact the insurance provider for clarification.


Corporate Health Plan Claim Process

The claim process for a Corporate Health plan can vary depending on the insurance provider and the specific policy. However, here is a general overview of the typical claim process:

  1. Inform the insurance provider: The policyholder should inform the insurance provider about the hospitalization as soon as possible.

  2. Submit the required documents: The policyholder should gather the required documents, such as a hospital discharge summary, bills and receipts, and a claim form, and submit them to the insurance provider.

  3. Cashless facility: If the policyholder is availing of a cashless facility at a network hospital, they should inform the hospital and the insurance provider and follow the procedures outlined by the insurance provider.

  4. Reimbursement: If the policyholder is not availing of a cashless facility, they should pay the hospital bills and then submit the required documents to the insurance provider for reimbursement.

  5. Review and processing of claim: The insurance provider will review the claim and the supporting documents to determine if the policyholder is eligible for coverage. If the claim is approved, the insurance provider will process the payment.

  6. Appeal: If the claim is denied, the policyholder can appeal the decision by following the appeals process outlined by the insurance provider.

It is important to carefully review the claim process for a specific Corporate Health plan and to follow all instructions provided by the insurance provider to ensure a smooth and efficient claim process. If the policyholder is unsure about the claim process or has questions, they can contact the insurance provider for assistance.


Documents Required for Claim Process

The documents required for the claim process of a Corporate Health plan can vary depending on the insurance provider and the specific policy. However, here is a list of common documents that may be required:

  • Claim form: A claim form, which is a formal request for payment of benefits under the policy.

  • Hospital discharge summary: A hospital discharge summary, which is a document provided by the hospital that summarizes the policyholder's hospital stay and treatment.

  • Bills and receipts: Original bills and receipts for medical expenses incurred, including hospitalization, doctor's fees, diagnostic tests, and medication.

  • Identification proof: A government-issued identification proof, such as a passport, PAN card, or Aadhaar card.

  • Medical reports: Medical reports, such as X-rays, test results, and consultant reports, as required by the insurance provider.

  • Insurance policy documents: A copy of the policyholder's insurance policy and any other related documents.

It is important to carefully review the list of required documents for a specific Corporate Health plan and to gather all necessary documents before submitting a claim. If the policyholder is unsure about the required documents, they can contact the insurance provider for clarification.


How Long Does it Take to Pay Out the Claim?

The time it takes for a Corporate Health plan claim to be paid out can vary depending on several factors, including the insurance provider, the complexity of the claim, and the completeness of the required documentation.

However, on average, a claim may take 7-14 working days to be processed and paid out once all required documentation has been received and reviewed by the insurance provider. If there are any issues or discrepancies in the documentation, the process may take longer.

It is important to carefully review the policy and understand the claim process and timelines outlined by the insurance provider. If the policyholder is unsure about the expected time frame for payment, they can contact the insurance provider for clarification.


Renewal Process

The renewal process for a Corporate Health plan typically involves the policyholder paying the premium on time to keep the policy in force. The renewal process can vary depending on the insurance provider and the specific policy, but here is a general overview:

  • Renewal notice: The policyholder will typically receive a renewal notice before the policy expires, which will include the premium amount due and the due date.

  • Payment of premium: The policyholder can pay the premium by the due date, either online or offline, as per the payment methods offered by the insurance provider.

  • Confirmation of renewal: After the premium has been paid, the policyholder will receive a confirmation of renewal, which will state the effective date of the renewed policy.

  • Review of policy: Before renewing the policy, the policyholder may want to review the policy to ensure that it meets their current needs and that the coverage and benefits are adequate.

It is important to renew the policy on time to avoid a break in coverage and to ensure that the policyholder and their employees are protected in the event of a medical emergency. If the policyholder is unsure about the renewal process or has questions, they can contact the insurance provider for assistance.
