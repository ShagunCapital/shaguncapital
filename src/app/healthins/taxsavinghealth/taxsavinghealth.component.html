What is Tax Saving Plan?

Individuals can claim tax deductions on their health insurance premiums under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act. The deductions are available for both individual and family health insurance plans, as well as for senior citizen health insurance plans.

For the tax year 2020-2021, a maximum of INR 25,000 can be claimed as a deduction for health insurance premiums for self, spouse and children. If the taxpayer or his/her spouse is a senior citizen, the maximum deduction increases to INR 30,000.

It's also worth noting that in addition to claiming deductions on health insurance premiums, individuals can also claim deductions for preventive health check-ups under Section 80D. The maximum deduction for preventive health check-ups is INR 5,000.

It's important to keep in mind that the deductions under Section 80D can only be claimed if the premiums are paid through cash, cheque or digital mode and the insurance company should issue a health insurance policy in the name of the individual or his/her family members.

It's always good to consult with a tax professional or to check the official website of the Income Tax Department of India for the latest information and to ensure that you're taking full advantage of all the tax benefits available to you.


What all Tax Saving plan Covers?

Tax saving plans, also known as tax-saving investment instruments, are financial products that offer tax benefits under specific provisions of the tax code. These plans help individuals to save on their taxable income, thereby reducing their tax liability.

Typically, the following types of investments are considered as tax-saving plans in India:

  • Public Provident Fund (PPF)
  • Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
  • National Pension System (NPS)
  • Equity-Linked Saving Schemes (ELSS)
  • National Savings Certificate (NSC)
  • Life Insurance premium
  • 5-year fixed deposit with a bank or a post office

Each of these tax-saving instruments has different investment criteria, lock-in periods, and tax benefits. It's important to understand the specific provisions of each instrument and choose one that aligns with your investment goals, risk tolerance, and financial situation.


Tax Saving Plan Claim Process

The claim process for tax-saving plans depends on the type of instrument. However, some common steps are:

  • Public Provident Fund (PPF): The maturity amount can be claimed by the account holder upon completion of 15 years from the date of opening the account. The account holder can also opt for partial withdrawals after the completion of 7 years from the date of opening the account. The claim process involves submitting a claim form along with relevant documents to the concerned authorities.

  • Employee Provident Fund (EPF): The EPF balance can be claimed by the employee upon retirement, resignation, termination, or in the event of death. The claim process involves submitting a claim form along with relevant documents to the concerned authorities.

  • National Pension System (NPS): Upon reaching the age of 60, the NPS account holder can withdraw up to 60% of the accumulated pension wealth, with the remaining 40% being used to purchase an annuity. The claim process involves submitting a claim form along with relevant documents to the concerned authorities.

  • Equity-Linked Saving Schemes (ELSS): The maturity amount can be claimed upon completion of the lock-in period, which is typically 3 years. The claim process involves submitting a redemption request to the mutual fund company.

  • National Savings Certificate (NSC): The maturity amount can be claimed upon completion of the term of the certificate, which is typically 5 years. The claim process involves submitting a request to the post office or bank where the NSC was purchased.

  • Life Insurance premium: The claim process for life insurance policies depends on the type of policy and the reason for the claim. In general, a death claim involves submitting a claim form along with relevant documents to the insurance company.

  • 5-year fixed deposit with a bank or a post office: The maturity amount can be claimed upon completion of the term of the fixed deposit, which is typically 5 years. The claim process involves submitting a request to the bank or post office where the fixed deposit was opened.

It's important to check with the financial institution offering the tax-saving investment instrument for the specific claim process.


How Long Does it Take to Pay Out the Claim?

The time it takes to pay out a claim for a health insurance plan can vary depending on several factors such as the complexity of the claim, the completeness of the required documents, and the insurance company's internal processes.

On average, a simple and straightforward claim can be settled within 2-4 weeks of submitting all the necessary documents. For more complicated claims, the process can take longer, sometimes up to several months.

It is important to check with the insurance company for the specific timelines for claim settlement as different insurance companies have different processes and procedures. It is also important to keep in mind that the claims process can be expedited by submitting all the required documents in a timely and complete manner.
